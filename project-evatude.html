Evatude Native Android and iOS App

A guided meditation and self-hypnosis app for achieving positive change.

The Problem

For this student project, I was tasked with designing a native mobile app for both Android and iOS operating systems that solves a specific user problem.

I wanted to address the problems of negative thinking and insomnia that burden so many in our society. To address these issues, I designed an app called Evatude, a guided meditation and self-hypnosis app to help users achieve positive change.

The app allows users to search for and join guided meditation and hypnosis programs, as well as track their progress as they develop a consistent listening practice.

The Design Process

  • Competitive research
  • User flows
  • Branding
  • Low, mid, and high-fidelity wireframes for iOS and Android
  • Prototype for iOS and Android UI
  • User testing and compiled user feedback

Competitve Research

The competitor apps I analyzed were: Calm, Headspace, Harmony, JC Hypnosis, and Slumber.

I found several apps on the market for meditation, but the options for hypnosis are limited. I analyzed each app carefully and found patterns I could employ or improve upon in my own design.

Low and Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

I began with low-fidelity wireframes that showcased the basic app functions.

Design differences between each platform were taken into consideration and specified for each of the user flows. Some differences are outlined above.

I further explored screen categories, sequences, and states to ensure additional needs were addressed before moving on to high-fidelity wireframes.

KEY TAKEAWAY

My first revision grew the app from 8 screens to 22, which proved to be too unwieldy for the scope of this first iteration. I settled on 16 screens.

Branding, Photography, and Color Palette

I was inspired by the deep, dreamy hues of the Slumber app, but wanted to differentiate the Evatude brand from its competitors by using a distinct color palette. Most apps in this category use deep purples and blues. I opted for a natural palette of bright emerald, forest green, khakis and greys. Rich, saturated photos of nature complete the contemplative aesthetic.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

High-fidelity wireframes were created to express the brand’s dark aesthetic while still following each platform’s specific design guidelines.

Prototypes and User Testing

With high-fidelity wireframes complete, it was time to test with users of each platform. 5 users tested the app (2 Android users, 3 iOS users) and shared their feedback.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

Positive feedback:

  • They liked the overall aesthetic. Colors and layout are working well.
  • Text was legible and spacing works.
  • Flows were easy to follow and they knew where togo (when they could see full screens and navigation).

Negative feedback:

  • Some elements are missing on the prototypes
  • Navigation isn’t visible on all screens.
  • (Android) The dark mode-style design is nice, but on the main search screen, having the big category buttons with so much brightness makes it jarring on the eyes.
  • (Android) The white used for search bars looks like a mistake since the light tan/khaki is used elsewhere for lighter colored, filled elements.
  • (Android) The white text on top of the horizontally-scrollng carousel images is hard to read.

Changes I made in response to the feedback:

(all on the Android version)

  • I changed the main category buttons on the search screen to images with bold text overlay like I’d done on the iOS version.
  • I decided to nix the image carousel option and go with a more traditional horizontally-scrolling gallery with text below the images for better legibility.
  • I changed the color of the search bars to light tan/khaki so it matches other filled elements.
